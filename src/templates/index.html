rhea.framework

Team Leadership in Complex Environments.

Aims
  • Support personal reflection on leadership in teams
  • Support learning from experience with peers (across company borders)
  • Support learning from othersâ€™ experience (through collegial and expert feedback)
  • Increase of personal leadership qualities and capacities
  • Collect and share learnings in team leadership
How does it work?
When a significant event occurs in day-to-day practice, a written reflection (or flow) can be worked out. The reflection guideline provides hints and recommendations for flow structuring.

The reference model can be used to compare personal experiences with reference flows (retrieved from peer-reviewed journal articles). Interactions can be classified and labelled using normative categories.

Feedback from interested peers and experts can be retrieved in interpersonal reflection sessions (or flow sessions). These moderated peer learning sessions may help clarify significant interpersonal work situations. Also, in these sessions the reference model can be utilized to place experience descriptions alongside other flows. Normative categories may help to differentiate leadership team interactions.

Flow sessions are rigorously organized adhering to the flow session policy. The steps described in the flow session procedure description may guide through peer learning sessions, particularly in the beginning.
