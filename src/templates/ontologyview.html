General Information

This reference model comprises a collection of experience descriptions concerning interpersonal leadership interactions in teams (flows) that can

  • support team performance,
  • serve as anchors in personal reflection and learning processes,
  • be implemented along the Unified Process.

Model Schema

Central to the reference model are leadership team interaction experience descriptions (flows). Flows are connected to other flows in the reference model based on semantic relationships between flow keywords. By the help of crowd-sourcing each flow was categorized to leadership behaviours, (project) risks, and Unified Process project life-cycle phases. Flows were further attributed to basic emotions according to the NRC Word-Emotion Association Lexicon, version 0.92. Sentiments were evaluated using the Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner. Each flow is part of one core flow.

Core Flows

An initial collection of 78 flows was retrieved in a systematic literature review. Four core flows - or foundational topics of leadership team interaction - were distinguished based on community-detection and document similarity clusterings.

Prototype Team Identity

This core flow deals with how individuals of diverse backgrounds working together feel being valuable participants of a team.

Organize for Complexity

The main intent is to find ways to deal with - or rather welcome and deploy - complexity in business or work requirements, (technological) resources, stakeholder involvement and interpersonal team processes. This includes team opportunities such as remote team members or virtual teams.

Facilitate Team Cohesion

In a cohesive team, participants work jointly towards shared goals. The team satisfies the emotional needs of its members. Team members work together complementarily - collaboratively rather than individually. Team cohesion is a dynamic, multifaceted, interpersonal process.

Structure for Task Effectiveness

Teams work towards shared goals. This core flow spans approaches and tools to complete goals in time, budget and scope satisfying stakeholders and, succeeding, team members. As such, it is most specific to the business area the team operates in. It is about getting things done.
